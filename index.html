More Than a Name, A Way of Seeing.

For over 20 years, I lived life one salary credit at a time. Predictable. Structured. Safe. Today, I’m unlearning that rhythm—slowly, imperfectly—trying to earn my wings as a free bird. I find my anchors in three unlikely teachers: Birds, Reptiles, and Kids.

Lessons from , the Wild

Birds remind me how vast the sky can be when you choose to fly. Reptiles teach me the power of stillness, simplicity, and survival without excess. And kids… they keep me honest, Curious, Alive. They are the reason I keep showing up. The Indian wilderness has been my classroom and my refuge. As a field guide, I’ve worked with forest departments across states— training local communities and tribal groups in the art of seeing, understanding, and sharing nature. I’ve walked through forests counting birds at dawn, but what stays with me most are the moments when a child spots their first bird and their world quietly expands. Parallel to this, I inhabit the corporate world—less as a resident, more as a translator between structure and possibility. I design learning experiences for teams and advise Banks and NBFCs on retail lending, with a focus on risk and policy. It’s a different jungle, but the patterns aren’t all that different.

I also write—because some journeys refuse to stay silent. My work has found space in Outlook Traveller yearbooks and Nature in Focus. My wildlife travel venture has been featured by Lonely Planet among India’s recommended names for birdwatching. This website is not a portfolio. It’s a trail... Of places I’ve been, people I’ve met, and stories that chose me along the way.
